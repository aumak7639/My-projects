If your company\u2019s website traffic is down and you aren\u2019t receiving the visibility you want, chances are that it\u2019s time to reevaluate your site\u2019s search engine optimization (SEO) to help boost your traffic.<\/p>\n

1. Google Search Console<\/h6>\n

If you don\u2019t have this set up, you won\u2019t be able to determine if Google is having difficulty crawling and indexing your site.<\/p>\n

2. Toxic Links<\/h6>\n

While link building is essential, having too many toxic links can create problems. Toxic links are links to spam sites or links to websites that are only for the purpose of link building, and often these sites contain malware or spam.<\/p>\n

3. Broken Links And Redirects<\/h6>\n

If you have several 301 redirects or broken links, search engines will rate your site lower.<\/p>\n

4. Performance<\/h6>\n

Search engines heavily weigh website performance and site speed when rating sites. If your site has outdated plugins or too many plugins that are clashing, it can slow down performance speed.<\/p>\n

5. Reviews<\/h6>\n

Whether you get a positive or negative review online, it\u2019s essential for your business to respond. Clients heavily rely on online reviews when selecting service providers. Promptly responding to reviews is necessary, and since Google weighs reviews, it\u2019s essential to respond to any left on Google.
